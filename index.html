World Happiness Dashboard

Welcome to the World Happiness Dashboard, a comprehensive visual exploration into the dynamics of happiness across the globe. This interactive platform illuminates the multifaceted nature of happiness, demonstrating how geographical, economic, social, and health factors converge to shape the global landscape of well-being. Journey through vivid visualizations that reveal disparities and correlations, from the color gradients of happiness across continents to the intricate interplay of GDP, generosity, and contentment. Discover the elements that resonate with joy in various cultures and societies, and delve into data-driven stories that speak volumes about our collective pursuit of happiness.

Global Happiness Map

Embark on a journey across the global happiness landscape, unraveling how happiness scores are distributed worldwide. The color intensity in the map above depicts the happiness score of each country, providing a visual representation of global happiness disparities. We can clearly see that the darker the area, the higher the happiness score. Notably, Europe, America, Australia, and New Zealand exhibit darker colors, indicating higher happiness scores compared to other regions.

Delving into Happiness Factors

Unveil the correlations between GDP per Capita and happiness scores while exploring how other factors like generosity (size of the bubble) and region (color of the bubble) play roles in shaping happiness levels across countries. The bubble chart offers a more granular perspective, presenting a multidimensional view of the factors contributing to happiness. Most Western Europe countries, Australia, and New Zealand, for instance, display high happiness scores alongside high GDP, suggesting a potential relationship. Furthermore, countries with higher happiness scores tend to exhibit greater generosity.

Stacked Bars of Happiness Factors

Discover how different factors contribute to happiness across regions by delving into the stacked bars. Each color represents a unique factor, providing insights into what drives happiness globally. This chart distinctly shows how average happiness, economy, family, health, and freedom contribute to overall happiness. Regions with higher happiness scores generally exhibit higher scores in these categories as well.

Happiness Score Distribution

Explore the distribution of happiness scores across regions through the boxplot. The boxplot unveils the spread and central tendency of happiness scores, revealing regional disparities and similarities. While Australia and New Zealand are not separately depicted due to proximity, they share a high median happiness score of 7.3, on par with North America. The median score is particularly insightful, highlighting the overall happiness level of a region, as evidenced by Sub-Saharan Africa's lower scores. In regions with a wide range of scores, this diversity significantly impacts the overall regional score.
